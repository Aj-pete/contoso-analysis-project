# intermidiate sql- sales analysis
## 1. Overview

This project analyzes customer purchasing behavior using sales and cohort data to answer key business questions:

#### -Customer Segmentation
#### -Cohort Analysis
#### -Retention Analysis
#### -Repeat purchase rate

The analysis uses SQL to segment customers, evaluate cohort performance, and measure retention trends. The goal is to extract actionable business insights that can improve revenue growth, customer retention, and marketing efficiency.

## 2. Customer Segmentation Analysis
Business Question

Who are our most valuable customers?

Methodology

Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) was calculated by summing the total net revenue generated by each customer.

Customers were segmented using percentile distribution
This segmentation helps identify which customers contribute the most revenue.

query: [1_customer_segmentation.sql](/1_customer_segmentation.sql)

### Visual; 
![customer segmentation](/imagess/1_customer_segementation.png)

### Key Findings 

Revenue concentration is  heavily skewed toward the high-value segment(66%-$135.4m).
A small percentage of customers generate the largest portion of revenue.

Medium-value customers represent the largest customer base(24,743 customers).
This group contributes consistent revenue(32%-66.6m) but has potential for growth.

Low-value customers contribute minimal revenue(2%-$4.3m) individually but may represent a large share of the customer base.

Average LTV varies significantly across segments, indicating strong differences in purchasing behavior.

### Business Insights

The business likely follows a Pareto-like revenue distribution, where a small group of customers drives the majority of revenue.

High-value customers are critical to revenue stability and should be prioritized in retention strategies.

Medium-value customers represent the largest opportunity for revenue expansion through targeted upselling.

Low-value customers may be new customers, one-time buyers, or price-sensitive shoppers.

### Strategic Recommendations

#### 1. Prioritize High-Value Customer Retention

Strategies:

VIP loyalty programs,
Personalized marketing,
Early access to promotions and
Exclusive product offerings

Reason: Losing high-value customers would significantly impact revenue.

#### 2. Upsell Medium-Value Customers

Strategies:

Product bundling,
Targeted cross-selling,
Loyalty incentives,
Subscription or membership offers

Reason: This segment represents the highest growth potential.

#### 3. Convert Low-Value Customers into Repeat Buyers

Strategies:

Post-purchase email campaigns,
First-to-second purchase incentives,
Retargeting ads,
Discount offers

Reason: Increasing purchase frequency from this group increases overall revenue.

## 4. Retention Analysis
Business Question;
Which customers haven't purchased recently?

Methodology

The analysis identified each customer's most recent purchase date.

Customers were classified as:

Active Customers: Purchased within the last 6 months.

Churned Customers: No purchase within the last 6 months.

Only customers who made their first purchase more than six months ago were evaluated to avoid incorrectly labeling new customers as churned.

query: [3_retention_analysis.sql](/3_retention_analysis.sql)

### visual;

[retention analysis](/imagess/3_customer_churn_cohort_year.png)

### Key Findings
Retention rates are consistently low (8-10%) across all cohorts, indicating retention issues are systemic rather than specific to certain years.

Newer cohorts (2022-2023) show similar churn trajectories indicating future cohorts will follow the same pattern.

Customers who have not purchased in the past 6 months represent a significant revenue recovery opportunity.


Some cohorts show higher churn rates, indicating:

Poor retention strategies during certain periods

Differences in acquisition quality.

Active customers represent the core recurring revenue base.

These customers are essential to sustaining revenue without relying heavily on new acquisitions.

### Business Insights

Ensure early engagement strategies to target the first 1-2 years with onboarding incentives, loyalty rewards, and personalized offers to improve long-term retention.

Retention performance varies based on acquisition year, indicating changes in marketing strategies or customer quality over time.

Recovering churned customers is typically cheaper than acquiring new ones.

### Strategic Recommendations

#### 1. Launch Win-Back Campaigns

Target churned customers with:

Personalized emails,
Exclusive discounts,
Limited-time offers

#### 2. Identify Churn Drivers

Further analysis should examine:

Products purchased,
Purchase frequency,
Average order value


#### 3. Implement Predictive Churn Monitoring

Use behavioral signals such as:

declining purchase frequency,
reduced engagement,
cart abandonment 
to intervene before customers churn.

## 5. Overall Strategic Insights

Across all analyses, several patterns emerge:

1. Customer Retention is a Major Opportunity

Revenue appears to depend heavily on initial purchases rather than repeat behavior.

Improving retention could significantly increase Customer Lifetime Value.

2. High-Value Customers Drive Revenue

Protecting and nurturing this segment should be a top strategic priority.

3. Medium-Value Customers Represent the Largest Growth Lever

With proper targeting and personalization, this group could become future high-value customers.

4. Churn Prevention is Critical

Customers who have not purchased in six months represent lost revenue potential.

Proactive retention strategies could significantly improve long-term revenue.
